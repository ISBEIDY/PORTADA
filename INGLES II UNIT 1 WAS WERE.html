UNIT 1 SIMPLE PAST OF THE VERB TO BE "WAS/WERE"



THERE WAS

-> Significa habia. Para singular y pasado

  • There was a cat. / Habia un gato.

  • There wasn't a cat. / No habia un gato.



    • THERE WERE

    -> Significa habia. Para plural y pasado

  • There were two cats. / Habia dos gatos.

  • There weren't three cats. / No habia tres gatos.



    • STRUCTURE

  • STRUCTURE

    • S + Aux. Verb + P. Verb + ing + C

    I was

    You
    We were
    They

    He was
    She

  • EXAMPLES
    • 1. John was at home last week.
    2. They were at the cinema yesterday.
    3. Your parents were at the station at nine o'clock.
    4. Francisco was in the street this morning.
    5. My aunt was in hospital yesterday morning.

    STRUCTURE

  • STRUCTURE

    • S + Aux. Verb + not + P. Verb + ing + C

    I wasn't

    You
    We weren't
    They

    He wasn't
    She

  • EXAMPLES
    • 1. Mum wasn't at home this morning.
    2. Paul and Mary weren't in the shop.
    3. His friends weren't very happy yesterday afternoon.
    4. I wasn't late for the cinema.
    5. We weren't at home to watch a film on TV.

    No, they weren't. They were at work.
